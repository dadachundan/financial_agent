# Risk Taxonomy — 8–12 Risks Across 4 Buckets

Each risk: 50–100 word description. For each, describe the risk clearly, quantify impact if possible, note likelihood/severity, identify mitigating factors.

## Company-Specific Risks (4–6 risks)
- Execution risk (can management deliver?)
- **Customer concentration — always evaluate; include as a risk whenever top-1 > 10% or top-5 > 30%.** Quantify top-1 % and top-5 % from disclosed filings (see SKILL.md Step 2 for where each domicile discloses this). Note the 3-year trend, the contract structure (multi-year master agreement vs. PO-by-PO), and whether any top customer is also a competitor or insourcing the product. Thresholds for severity: top-1 > 20% or top-5 > 50% is **material**; top-1 > 30% or single customer drives the business is **high**. If disclosure is absent, state that explicitly rather than omitting the risk.
- Key person dependency
- Product / technology obsolescence
- Geographic concentration
- Supplier concentration (mirror of customer concentration — single-source inputs, foundry exclusivity, IP licensing from one vendor)
- Integration risk (if recent M&A)

## Industry / Market Risks (3–4 risks)
- Competitive intensity
- Regulatory changes
- Technology disruption
- Market saturation

## Financial Risks (2–3 risks)
- Profitability timeline
- Funding requirements
- Debt levels and covenants
- Cash burn rate (if unprofitable)

## Macroeconomic Risks (2–3 risks)
- Economic sensitivity (cyclical vs. defensive)
- Interest rate sensitivity
- Foreign exchange exposure
- Geopolitical factors
